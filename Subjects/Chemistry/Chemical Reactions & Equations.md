### Balancing Chemical Equations
> It is the method of equalising number of atoms on the reactant side and product side.

> [!question] What is the need of balancing a chemical equation?
> According to the <mark style="background:rgba(240, 200, 0, 0.2)">Law of Conservation of Mass</mark>, total mass before reaction must be equal to total mass after reaction. To satisfy the law of conservation of mass, we have to make the number of atoms equal on both the sides.

**Examples:**
* $$3Fe + 4H_{2}O \rightarrow Fe_{3}O_{4} + 4H_{2}$$
* $$CH_{4} + 2O_{2} \rightarrow CO_{2} + 2H_{2}O$$

> [!example]+ Balanced chemical equations
> 1. $$4Na + O_{2} \rightarrow 2Na_{2}O$$
> 2. $$2Mg + O_{2} \rightarrow 2MgO$$
> 3. $$4Al + 3O_{2} \rightarrow 2Al_{2}O_{3}$$
> 4. $$2NaOH + H_{2}SO_{4} \rightarrow Na_{2}SO_{4} + 2H_{2}O$$

### Types of Reactions

#### Combination Reaction
> Reaction in which two or more reactants combine to form a single product.
> e.g. $$H_{2} + O_{2} \rightarrow H_{2}O$$

> [!note] Activity 1: Whitewashing
> Take a beaker, put some CaO powder in it. Mix it with water.
> **Observation:** Experienced heat while touching side of the beaker.
> $$CaO + H_{2}O \rightarrow Ca(OH)_{2} + Heat$$ *(Slaked Lime)*
> 

 >[!important]
> Solution of $$Ca(OH)_2$$ is $$CaO$$

> $$Ca(OH)_2 $$ is used for whitewashing

> [!info] Exothermic Reaction
> Reaction in which energy is released.
> e.g. $$CaO + H_{2}O \rightarrow Ca(OH)_{2} + Heat$$

> [!question] Why is respiration called exothermic?
> In respiration, the Glucose combine with Oxygen to form Carbondioxide, Water & Energy. Due to the release of energy respiration is called exothermic.
> $$C_{6}H_{12}O_{6} + 6O_{2} \rightarrow 6CO_{2} + 6H_{2}O + Energy$$

#### Decomposition Reaction
> Reaction in which a single reactant is splitted into 2 or more products.

**1. Thermal Decomposition:** Decomposition using heat energy.
* $$CaCO_{3} \xrightarrow{\Delta} CaO + CO_{2}$$
* $$2FeSO_{4} \cdot 7H_{2}O \xrightarrow{\Delta} 2FeSO_{4} + 14H_{2}O \xrightarrow{\Delta} Fe_{2}O_{3} + SO_{2} + SO_{3}$$ *(Pale green $\rightarrow$ Brown)*
* $$2Pb(NO_{3})_{2} \xrightarrow{\Delta} 2PbO + 4NO_{2} + O_{2}$$ *(Brown fumes)*

**2. Photodecomposition:** Decomposition using light energy.
* $$2AgCl_{(aq)} \xrightarrow{sunlight} 2Ag + Cl_{2}$$ *(White $\rightarrow$ Grey)*
* $$2AgBr_{(aq)} \xrightarrow{sunlight} 2Ag + Br_{2}$$ *(Yellow $\rightarrow$ Grey)*
* *Note: This is used in B/W photography.*

**3. Electrodecomposition (Electrolysis):** Decomposition using electricity.
* *e.g. Electrolysis of Acidified water*
* $$2H_{2}O \xrightarrow{electricity} 2H_{2} + O_{2}$$
* *H will be collected at Cathode & O will be collected at anode.*

> [!important] 
> The amount of H gas collected will be double than that of Oxygen because in a water molecule the ratio between H & O is 2:1.

> [!info] Endothermic Reaction
> It is the reaction in which energy is absorbed. All decomposition reactions are endothermic.

---

#### Displacement Reaction
> It is the reaction in which a more reactive metal displaces a less reactive metal from its compound.

* $$CuSO_{4} + Fe \rightarrow FeSO_{4} + Cu$$ *(Blue $\rightarrow$ Pale green)*
* $$CuSO_{4} + Zn \rightarrow ZnSO_{4} + Cu$$

> [!question] Can we store Copper Sulphate Solution in an Iron container?
> No, we cannot store Copper Sulphate in an Iron container because Iron displaces Copper in Copper sulphate and the solution changes to $$FeSO_{4}$$.

---

#### Double Displacement Reaction
> Reaction in which exchange of ions take place between two compounds.

* $$BaCl_{2(aq)} + Na_{2}SO_{4(aq)} \rightarrow BaSO_{4(s)}\downarrow + 2NaCl_{(aq)}$$ *(White precipitate)*
* $$NaOH_{(aq)} + HCl_{(aq)} \rightarrow NaCl_{(aq)} + H_{2}O_{(l)}$$

> [!info] Precipitation Reaction
> Reaction in which a precipitate is formed.
> $$Pb(NO_{3})_{2(aq)} + 2KI_{(aq)} \rightarrow PbI_{2}\downarrow + 2KNO_{3(aq)}$$ *(Yellow precipitate)*

### Oxidation & Reduction
* **Oxidation** is the reaction in which Oxygen is added or Hydrogen is removed.
* **Reduction** is the reaction in which Oxygen is removed or Hydrogen is added.
==(Diagram: Connect CuO to Cu for "Reduction" and H2 to H2O for "Oxidation")==
$$CuO + H_{2} \rightarrow Cu + H_{2}O$$
- Oxidation ($H_{2}$) $\rightarrow$ Reducing Agent
- Reduction ($CuO$) $\rightarrow$ Oxidising Agent

==(Diagram: Connect PbO to Pb for "Reduction" and C to CO2 for "Oxidation")==
$$2PbO + C \rightarrow 2Pb + CO_{2}$$
- Oxidation ($C$) $\rightarrow$ Reducing Agent
- Reduction ($PbO$) $\rightarrow$ Oxidising Agent

==(Diagram: Connect Fe2O3 to Fe for "Reduction" and Al to Al2O3 for "Oxidation")==
$$Fe_{2}O_{3} + 2Al \rightarrow Al_{2}O_{3} + 2Fe$$

==(Diagram: Connect MnO2 to MnCl2 for "Reduction" and HCl to Cl2 for "Oxidation")
$$MnO_{2} + 4HCl \rightarrow MnCl_{2} + 2H_{2}O + Cl_{2}$$

### Rancidity & Corrosion

<center><h3>Rancidity</h3></center>
> It is the condition in which oil or fat content materials undergo oxidation to develop foul smell and taste.

**Methods to prevent rancidity:**
* Store in air tight containers
* Flush nitrogen in the packets
* Adding anti-oxidants

---

<center><h3>Corrosion</h3></center>
 
 > [!note]
> It is the reaction in which metals combine with oxygen, carbondioxide, water etc. to form compounds
>   *e.g. Iron react with Oxygen and water to form rust.*
> 

**Methods to prevent rust:**
* Painting
* Oiling / Greasing
* Galvanizing (Coating Zn over Iron)
* Electroplating (Coating metal over other)
* Alloy making
