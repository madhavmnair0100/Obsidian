### Question & Answers

> [!question] What is the underlining idea of the poem 'Fire and Ice'?
> The poet compares fire with **desire** and ice with **hatred**. Both of these are growing at an enormous speed. If we don't check them from growing, the world will perish. So we must restrain from desires and hatred to love our fellow beings.

> [!question] Which two ideas about how the world will end have been mentioned in the poem? Which idea does the poet support more?
> The two ideas mentioned are fire and ice. Though the poet thinks both are great for destruction, he seems to favor the idea of <mark style="background:rgba(255, 133, 0, 0.2)">fire</mark> a little more than ice.
