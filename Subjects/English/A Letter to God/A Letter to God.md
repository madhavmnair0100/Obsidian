## A Letter to God

### Question & Answers

> [!question] What did Lencho hope for and why?
> Lencho hoped for a <mark style="background:rgba(240, 200, 0, 0.2)">good rain / a downpour / a shower</mark>. By this he expects his crops would give him good yields.

> [!question] Why did Lencho write a letter? What did he ask for?
> When Lencho's annual crop was completely destroyed due to heavy rain and hailstorm, there was no one to help him during the crisis. So he wrote a letter to God requesting him to send **hundred pesos**, so that he could sow his field again and look after his entire family for the year.

> [!question] Why did Lencho say the raindrops were like "new coins"?
> Lencho hoped to get good crops because of raindrops. He thought he would earn money from the harvest, so he called them <mark style="background:rgba(255, 133, 0, 0.2)">new coins</mark>.

> [!question] What were Lencho's feelings when the hailstorm stopped?
> Lencho was filled with grief and sadness. The hailstorm had destroyed his crop completely. He told his family that their work had come to nothing and they would have to go hungry that year.

> [!question] Who read the letter and what did he do then?
> The **postmaster** read the letter. He was astonished at the faith Lencho had in God. He thought of helping Lencho in order not to shake the writer's faith. He collected money from his employees and friends and gave a part of his salary.

### Character Sketch of Lencho

> [!info] Lencho is the protagonist of the story 'A Letter to God'.
> - He was a **dedicated farmer** who worked like an ox in his fields.
> - He was a man of <mark style="background:rgba(0, 255, 255, 0.2)">unwavering faith</mark> in God.
> - When his crops were destroyed, he didn't give up hope and decided to write a letter to God.
> - He was a simple and innocent man who couldn't even imagine that humans (post office employees) were the ones who helped him, eventually calling them a "bunch of crooks."
