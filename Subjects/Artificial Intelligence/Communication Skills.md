## What is Communication?

Communication involves **Speaking**, **Listening** & **Understanding**.

Communication has **3 parts**:

| Part | Full Term | Description |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **T** | Transmitting | Sender conveys or transmits a message to another person or a group of persons through a medium of communication. |
| **L** | Listening | The receiver receives a message through a medium of communication & understands the message. |
| **F** | Feedback | It is the response/reaction of the person to the sender after understanding the message. |

## 1. Verbal Communication

### Sub-types of Verbal Communication

#### a) Interpersonal Communication
- In this form of communication, info is exchanged between **two individuals**.
- To communicate effectively, two individuals **interchange their roles** of sender and receiver repeatedly.
- **eg:** Talking with friends or family

#### b) Written Communication
- It is the communication through **written words**.
- Writing is **more valid and reliable** than speech.
- It has **legal validity**.
- **eg:** Letters, reports, memos, questionnaires, manuals, WhatsApp, iMessage

#### c) Public Communication
- This type of communication takes place when an **individual addresses a large gathering** of people.
- **eg:** Public speeches

#### d) Small Group Communication
- It occurs when there are **two or more people** involved.
- Number of people involved is **small**.
- It allows each participant to **interact and converse** with the rest.
- In this case **only one person speaks** at a time and others listen.
- **eg:** Board meetings, press conference, tele-conference

### Developing Communication Skills (Mastering)

To enhance our communication skills, we need to develop some basic traits:

> [!tip] Traits to Master
> Speech → Vocabulary → Rhythm → Tone → Pitch → Think → Confidence

#### Speech
It is the act of delivering a **formal oral communication** to an intended audience.

**Clarity** is essential and is needed most while communicating. For this, one should use **appropriate words** and speak with **clean diction and pronunciation**.

#### Vocabulary
Effective communication is judged by the **correct usage of vocabulary**. If one is not sure of how to use a word, one should **avoid using it**.

Vocabulary can be improved by **learning new words daily**. Consult a dictionary to learn how to pronounce a new word.

#### Rhythm
Rhythm is the **intonation** that is variation in the **pitch level of voice**. One should maintain a rhythm to express one's attitude and different array of emotions, such as surprise, happiness, irritation, curiosity, importance etc.

#### Tone
Words derive their power from **the way they are spoken**. The tone of the speaker helps to **create an impact** on the listener. It reflects the **attitude and emotions** of the speaker.

#### Pitch
It refers to the **loudness and softness** of a speaker's voice. One should always speak in a **clear, slow and conversational tone** in order to be an effective communicator.

#### Think
It is necessary to **think before you speak**. Plan and think about the topic so that the listener can understand it in an **effective way**.

#### Confidence
It is a **crucial skill** that can greatly impact **personal and professional success**. Maintain eye contact, stand straight, use gestures and display friendliness to look confident.

## 2. Non-Verbal Communication

It happens in the **absence of any oral or composed words**. It depends on different non-verbal prompts like **body movements, gestures, facial expression, symbol, image, signal, chart** etc. to express sentiments, attitudes or information.

### Importance

| % | Channel |
|---|---------|
| **55%** | Body language — facial expression, movement of arms, body movements etc. |
| **38%** | Vocal elements — Tone, pitch, pause |
| **7%** | Actual words spoken |

---

### Effective Non-Verbal Communication

- Use a variety of **appropriate facial expressions** while speaking.
- Try to make **eye contact** while speaking in the classroom, during presentation, discussion etc. — as eye contact defines **confidence and willingness to connect**.
- Make **variations in your vocal elements** throughout the presentation and avoid a **monotonous vocal rate**.
- Avoid **defensive gestures** like holding hands in front of your chest or putting them behind your back.
- **Refrain from fidgeting** with your hands or clothes. During a presentation always **stand straight** and move around with **ease and assurance**.

---

### Functions of Non-Verbal Communication

- We use NVC to **reinforce** VC.
	- **eg:** A nod/head shake reinstates the verbal message of 'Yes/No'
- We use NVC to **replace** VC using behaviours that are easily recognised by others, such as a wave, a nod or a head shake.
- We use NV Cues to **communicate emotions**.
	- **eg:** It is easy to spot when your friend is feeling sad. Her drooping shoulders would be indicative of her emotion.
- We use NVC to **give feedback** to other person during a discussion. If the audience is smiling or nodding, it shows that they agree with the speaker.

---

### NV Practices During an Interview

- Maintain **eye contact** with interviewer for a few seconds at a time.
- **Smile and nod** at appropriate times when the interviewer is talking.
- Be **polite** and keep an **even tone** to your speech.
- Don't be **too loud or too quiet** during speech.
- **Do not slouch** while sitting.
- **Relax and lean** a little towards the interviewer so that you appear interested and engaged.
- **Do not lean back**.
- Keep your **feet flat on the floor** and your back against the **lower back of the chair**.

### Types of NVC

> [!tip] Mnemonic — Types of NVC
> **"Funny Giants Play Ball Every Tuesday Past Sunset"**
> F — Facial Expression | G — Gestures | P — Paralanguage | B — Body Language | E — Eye Contact | T — Touch | P — Posture | S — Space

#### Facial Expression
Facial expressions are our expressions used to convey **emotions and reactions** such as anger, happiness etc. Use **appropriate expression** with context to speech.

- They are the **most noticeable aspect** of NVC.
- They are often the **first thing people see**, even before they hear what the speaker has to say.

**Q: How can it be used?**
- Smile to convey **approachability**.
- Ensure your expressions **match your words**.
- Keep your **face relaxed**.

---

#### Gestures
Gestures are **movements of hands or arm/head** to express ideas. Hand and body movement signals are essential ways to convey words.

**Q: How can it be used?**
- Wave, point and use fingers to **indicate numbers**.
- **Nod** while indicating agreement and understanding.
- Use hand gestures to add **clarity** to your message.

---

#### Paralanguage
NV Speech such as **tone, pitch, volume modulation, rhythm and rate** are essential elements of communication. These non-verbal sounds provide **subtle but powerful clues** into our true feelings and what we mean.
- **eg:** Tone of voice can indicate anger, affection or confidence.

**Q: How can it be used?**
- **Adjust your tone and volume** according to the message you want to convey.
- Speak clearly and at a **moderate volume** so that the message is received well.

---

#### Body Language
Body language includes **facial expression, eye movements, hand gestures, posture** etc. Using appropriate expressions and gestures **strengthens your speech**.

---

#### Eye Contact
The movement of eyes such as **looking, staring & blinking** is an essential form of NVC.
- Eye gaze can also help determine if someone is being **honest**.
- Steady eye contact can be interpreted as a sign of **truthfulness & trustworthiness**.

**Q: How can it be used?**
- **Look directly** at the person speaking.
- Ensure proper eye contact to show **engagement and confidence**.

---

#### Touch
Communicating through **physical contact** is another essential form of NV Behaviour. Sometimes touch can express feelings **more effectively than words**.

- **eg:** A warm hug from a mother consoles a child.
- A handshake can convey **confidence**.
- A pat on the back or hug can show **support**.

**Q: How can it be used?**
- **Shake hands** when meeting someone.
- Use appropriate touch to convey **sympathy, support or congratulations**.

---

#### Posture
It refers to **how we position our bodies**. Good posture conveys **confidence, attentiveness and openness**.
- Poor posture can indicate **fatigue, lack of interest or even low self esteem**.

**Q: How can it be used?**
- Ensure your spine **maintains its natural curve**.
- Keep your **shoulders straight**.

---

#### Space
Space refers to the **physical distance** between two people. Respecting personal space can help **avoid discomfort and build trust**.
- Adjust your distance depending on the relationship — whether **formal or informal**.

## Communication Cycle

Communication is the **transfer of ideas or information** through a communication medium.

In the communication process, one person is a **sender** who conveys a message to another person known as the **receiver**. When the receiver gets the message, he acknowledges it and sends back a response. This leads to a **cyclic process** known as the **communication cycle**.

A communication cycle includes resources such as **email threads, social media outlets, VoIP** (Voice over Internet Protocol) **message outlets** etc.

<p align="center" style="font-size: 10px; color: gray;">(Insert Diagram: Communication Cycle — Sender → Encoding → Communication Channel → Decoding → Receiver → Feedback → back to Sender)</p>

### Elements of Communication Cycle

**→ Sender**
A person who expresses his opinion, idea or message and **sends it to the receiver**.

**→ Message**
It refers to the **information** that the sender wishes to convey.

**→ Encoding**
It refers to **how the sender wishes to communicate** the message — using spoken, written or body language.

**→ Communication Channel**
It is a **medium** through which the message is carried and sent to others.

**→ Receiver**
A person **for whom the message is intended**.

**→ Decoding**
It refers to how the receiver **interprets** the message and translates it into thoughts, depending on his circumstances and understanding.

**→ Feedback**
It is the **response** that a receiver gives after the message is received and understood by him. It is the **final component** in the cycle of communication.

**Q: What is Feedback?**
It is an essential factor in the process of the communication cycle. It is the **response or the reaction** of the receiver to the sender after perceiving or understanding the message. After gaining the receiver's response, the sender takes further actions, thereby **completing the communication cycle**.

## Feedback

### Importance of Feedback

> [!tip] Mnemonic — Importance of Feedback
> **"People Eat Many Pizza Like Pepperoni"**
> P — Problem solving | E — Effective listening | M — Motivation | P — Performance | L — Learning (tool) | P — Present (always)

**→ Basis of Problem Solving**
When the receiver gets a message, it is up to him to agree with the sender or not. If the receiver disagrees, it can cause a problem. In this case the **feedback can help the sender to justify the response** of the receiver.

**→ Promote Effective Listening**
Feedback results in **effective listening**. When feedback is given, the sender becomes cautious of the message sent by him. He **reanalyses this message and corelates it with the feedback**.

**→ Provides Motivation**
Students need motivation to study and perform the given tasks well. **Right feedback given at the right time** can do wonders for them.

**→ Improves Performance**
Good performance is what everybody strives for. It can take you to great heights. For that you need to **overcome your fears, shortcomings and worries**.

**→ Tool for Continued Learning**
Feedback plays an important role in learning. Proper feedback **prevents you from getting distracted** and pushes you to remain **aligned to your goals**.

**→ Always Present**
When you speak to people, feedback is **communicated inherently by nature**, making it impossible to not provide feedback.

### Descriptive Feedback

Descriptive Feedback includes **specific information** in the form of **written comments or verbal conversation** that help the learner understand what he/she needs to do in order to improve.

> [!abstract] According to Black, Harrison, Lee and William
> "Descriptive feedback is the most powerful tool for improving students' learning."

#### Benefits of Descriptive Feedback

1. **Provides useful information** to students about their learning.
2. **Fills the gap** between their present level of performance and learning goals.
3. **Enables students to self-assess** and set their goals accordingly.

#### Factors Essential for Providing Descriptive Feedback

1. **Goal Oriented** — Feedback should be given keeping the **aim of learning** in mind as students work towards achieving the learning objective.

2. **Actionable** — When students have knowledge but do not know where to apply it, they lose interest in learning. Hence, their experience should be put to use by giving them **specific direction** and avoiding general comments.

3. **User Friendly** — The feedback should be given in an **easy to understand language**, so that students can comprehend and achieve the learning aim.

4. **Timely** — The students should receive the feedback **on time** — that is, when it is still possible for them to put it to use.

5. **Continuous** — Feedback should be given **continuously along with instructions**. This enables the student to remain focused on their goal.

6. **Consistent** — The student should be given **consistent feedback**. Otherwise, they will not be able to rely on it and in turn will not be able to follow it.
