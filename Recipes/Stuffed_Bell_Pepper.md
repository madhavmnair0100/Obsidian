# OTG Stuffed Bell Pepper
> Paneer and potato stuffed pepper baked in the OTG. Uses up your leftover tikka paneer perfectly.

---

## Ingredients
*(Serves 1)*

| Ingredient | Amount |
| :--- | :--- |
| Bell pepper (whole) | 1 |
| Leftover paneer, crumbled or chopped small | 100g |
| Potato, boiled and mashed | 1 |
| Onion, finely chopped | 1 |
| Tomato, finely chopped | 1 |
| Oil | 1 tbsp |
| Red chilli powder | ½ tsp |
| Garam masala | ¼ tsp |
| Salt | ½ tsp |
| Chaat masala *(for finishing)* | ½ tsp |

---

## Steps

> [!note] Step 1 — Preheat OTG
> Preheat your OTG to **200°C for 10 minutes**. Place the rack in the **middle position**.

> [!note] Step 2 — Prep the Pepper
> Slice the **top off** the bell pepper and scoop out the seeds and white membrane. Rub a little oil on the outside. Set aside the cap — you'll bake it alongside.

> [!note] Step 3 — Make the Filling
> Combine paneer, mashed potato, onion, tomato, oil, chilli powder, garam masala and salt. Mix well.
> **Optional (better):** Sauté onion in oil first until soft → add tomato for 2 mins → mix in paneer and potato.

> [!note] Step 4 — Stuff and Bake
> Fill the pepper **tightly** with the mixture. Place it upright on a small baking tray. Put the cap back on top. Bake at **200°C for 20 minutes**.

> [!note] Step 5 — Finish and Serve
> Remove from OTG. Sprinkle **chaat masala** on top. Let it sit for **2 minutes** before eating — it'll be very hot inside.

---

> [!tip] 10L OTG Tips
> - Use a **small steel or aluminium tray**, not a large one.
> - If the pepper keeps falling over, slice a **thin layer off the bottom** to flatten it.
> - No cheese right now — but if you ever get some, stuff a cube **inside the filling** before baking. Game changer.
