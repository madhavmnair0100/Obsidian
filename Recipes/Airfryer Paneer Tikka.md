# 🍢 Airfryer Paneer Tikka

Smoky, charred paneer tikka with zero tandoor required. The airfryer does the work.

> [!info] At a Glance
> ⏱ Prep: 15 mins &nbsp;|&nbsp; 🔥 Cook: 15 mins &nbsp;|&nbsp; 🍽 Serves: 2

## 🧂 Ingredients

**Paneer & Veg**
- [ ] 200g paneer, cubed (1.5 inch pieces)
- [ ] 1 medium capsicum, chunked
- [ ] 1 medium onion, chunked

**Marinade**
- [ ] 4 tbsp thick curd
- [ ] 3 tbsp besan (or rice flour)
- [ ] 1 tbsp oil
- [ ] 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
- [ ] 1 tsp red chilli powder
- [ ] 1 tsp coriander powder
- [ ] 1 tsp garam masala
- [ ] 1 tsp chaat masala
- [ ] 1 tsp salt
- [ ] 1 tsp turmeric
- [ ] 1 lemon, juiced

--- column-break ---

## 👨‍🍳 Method

1. **Cut the paneer and vegetables** — Cut paneer into 1.5 inch cubes, roughly the size of a big dice. Uniform size is important — uneven pieces mean some burn while others stay raw. Cut capsicum and onion into similarly sized chunks and separate the onion layers.

2. **Make the marinade** — In a large bowl combine the curd, besan, oil, ginger-garlic paste, red chilli powder, coriander powder, garam masala, chaat masala, salt, turmeric, and lemon juice. Mix into a thick, smooth paste. It should be tangy, spicy, and slightly salty.

3. **Coat and marinate** — Add the paneer, capsicum, and onion to the bowl. Gently toss until every piece is fully coated. Be gentle — paneer crumbles easily. Cover and rest for at least 15 minutes. 30–60 minutes is better.

4. **Preheat the airfryer** — Set to 200°C and run empty for 3–4 minutes. This gives you the instant heat burst needed for charring.

5. **Airfry** — Place marinated pieces in a single layer in the basket. Leave gaps between pieces — crowding causes steaming, not charring. Cook at 200°C for 12–15 minutes, flipping halfway at the 6–7 minute mark.

6. **Serve** — Plate immediately. Sprinkle extra chaat masala and squeeze lemon over the top. Serve with green chutney and raw sliced onion on the side.

> [!tip] Tips & Swaps
> - **No besan?** Rice flour is actually crispier — use it freely.
> - **No chaat masala?** Extra lemon + a pinch of amchur (dry mango powder) does the job.
> - **No capsicum?** Skip it or double the onion — no big deal.
> - **Have skewers?** Thread paneer, capsicum, and onion alternately for even cooking and a better look.

> [!warning] Don't Do This
> - Don't use watery curd — it slides off the paneer instead of coating it.
> - Don't crowd the basket — always leave space or cook in two batches.
> - Don't cut paneer too small — it'll dry out completely.

> [!success] You Know It's Done When...
> The paneer looks golden and slightly blistered on the edges, and the capsicum and onion have visible char marks.
