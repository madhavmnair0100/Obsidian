# ☕ Cardamom-Honey Latte

A rich, creamy homemade latte with a strong espresso-style base and silky cardamom-infused Milma Prime foam. No steam wand needed.

> [!info] At a Glance
> ⏱ Prep: 5 mins &nbsp;|&nbsp; 🔥 Cook: 10 mins &nbsp;|&nbsp; 🍽 Serves: 3

## 🧂 Ingredients

**Espresso Base**
- [ ] 12 tsp instant coffee (4 tsp per person)
- [ ] 3 to 6 tsp honey (adjust to taste)
- [ ] 12 tsp boiling water

**Infused Milk**
- [ ] 3 large cups Milma Prime milk (600–700ml)
- [ ] 6 to 9 elaichi (cardamom) pods, lightly crushed
- [ ] 3 tsp white sugar (optional, helps stabilize foam)

--- column-break ---

## 👨‍🍳 Method

1. **Make the espresso base** — In a small bowl or jug, combine the 12 tsp instant coffee and honey. Add the 12 tsp boiling water. Stir vigorously until the coffee is fully dissolved and the liquid looks dark, thick, and glossy. Divide equally into 3 mugs and set aside.

2. **Infuse and heat the milk** — Pour the milk into a medium saucepan. Add the crushed elaichi pods and sugar. Heat on medium, watching closely — you want steam and tiny bubbles at the edges but absolutely no rolling boil. Once steaming hot, drop to the lowest heat and let it sit for 2 minutes so the cardamom fully infuses.

3. **Texture the milk** — Turn off the heat. Fish out the cardamom pods with a spoon or strain the milk into a fresh pot. Tilt the pot slightly and whisk rapidly using a side-to-side or circular motion. You want tiny microscopic bubbles — not large soapy ones. Whisk for about 60 seconds until the milk looks shiny, silky, and has increased slightly in volume. Work fast — foam separates quickly.

4. **Assemble** — Tilt one mug at an angle. Pour the textured milk slowly into the center of the coffee from about 4 inches above — this shoots the milk under the coffee layer. As the mug hits 3/4 full, bring the pot closer to the rim and pour slightly faster so the silky foam on top lands on the surface. Repeat for the other two mugs.

5. **Finish** — Tap each mug gently on the counter to pop any large bubbles. Garnish with a tiny pinch of crushed inner cardamom seeds over the foam center.

> [!tip] Tips & Swaps
> - **Why Milma Prime?** High fat content — the fats and proteins wrap around air bubbles creating a flat white / latte texture. Regular toned milk won't foam as well.
> - **Honey quantity** — Start with 3 tsp total across all 3 mugs. Taste the coffee base before dividing and adjust.
> - **No hand whisk?** A milk frother or even vigorous stirring with a fork works in a pinch.
> - **Want it stronger?** Use 5 tsp coffee per person instead of 4.

> [!warning] Don't Do This
> - Don't let the milk boil — it kills the foam and scorches the bottom.
> - Don't whisk and then wait — pour immediately after whisking or the foam separates.
> - Don't use large circular whisking strokes — you'll get big airy bubbles, not silky micro-foam.

> [!abstract] Why This Works
> Dissolving coffee in very little water (1:1 ratio) mimics the strength of a real espresso shot. Whisking hot Milma Prime while just below boiling lets the fats and proteins trap air, creating that heavy, luxurious flat white texture you usually only get with a steam wand.

> [!success] You Know It's Done When...
> The milk looks like shiny liquid silk, has slightly increased in volume, and the foam sits on top of the latte without immediately sinking.
